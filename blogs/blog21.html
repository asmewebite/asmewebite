The wheel reinvented

- By Mounish

A good tyre means a lot when the nearest assistance is a million miles away. As the missions to outer space searching for life on other planets, satellites are getting more and more complex by the day everything has to be made more intelligent. That includes the very basic wheel. During the recent times of travel of the rover curiosity on mars lot of damage was incurred on the rover wheels. It was clearly evident that there is a strong need for the development of wheels for the future missions.

One might wonder why it is necessary to develop a new wheel when we can use a normal wheel. It is not recommended because filling the tyre with air is a problem in space and adding a gas tank especially for this also increases the weight of the rover. The other problem is if the tyre gets punctured there will be no one around to fix it.

Things one needs to remember when one is making a rover wheel are, its weight, its strength and its resiliency. One such wheel was developed by NASA’s Glenn research centre using the shape memory alloy Nitinol. This a tyre totally balanced over raw strength of the material unlike normal types requiring air to hold the structure.

Nitinol is a nickel titanium alloy which has shape memory properties below a set temperature and super elastic properties above it. The shape memory is the property of the alloy which allows the material to get back to its set shape at a certain point of temperature called transformation temperature even when it’s totally deformed. And above this temperature it has super elastic properties meaning it can handle higher stresses than normal metals do. The transformation temperature can be set by varying the ratio of nickel and titanium. And the shape can be set by annealing at 500˚-600˚c and rapidly cooling it.

All the rover wheels used for mars missions up until now are rigid and have low elasticity so they get damaged easily. So, the nitinol wheels are one of the solutions because of their high elasticity. The nitinol mesh can handle up to 30 times the deformation of that of a conventional spring steel mesh.

When climbing sharp rocks the stress is increased at the point of contact making it more prone to deformation. At this point the rigid wheels are punctured but the nitinol wheels are deformed and reverted back to their original shape, thanks to its super elastic nature. They are almost acting like adding shock absorbers providing a smoother ride for the rover in the rocky terrain. The wheel is made of a spring mesh which allows the wheel to be lighter and have higher traction and durability.

With many qualities like super elasticity and shape memory its uses are not only limited to space. We may also expect these wheels to be on off road vehicles because they are air free hence no fear of getting a puncture. These are also used for medical purposes like in super elastic needles dentistry and stents. Interesting isn’t it, what do you think about it?

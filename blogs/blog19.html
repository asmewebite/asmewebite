Drones: Boon or Bane?

- By Atharva Gapchup

The day is not far when Domino’s delivering our pizzas by drones will become common and receiving our parcels from e-commerce websites at the windows will become a habit. Drones have gained popularity in a short span of time and are becoming an integral part of our life. They are being used in almost every industry, ranging from photography for taking aerial shots, to agriculture for spraying fertilizers and pesticides, and from military for vigilance, to online shopping for a fast delivery.


The main reason drones have become so popular and common is their low cost of manufacturing and ease of control. They can be easily deployed from any place in any condition and can take off and land vertically and have the ability to hover at a place. They also reduce the dependance on human resource on a large scale like losing a drone is better than losing a life of a precious soldier. But it may become a cause of concern as it may result in unemployment. They are also available in various shapes and sizes. Drones are so effective in whatever they do that all the planet exploration missions are accomplished by drones.


They have their own disadvantages also, recently there have been many instances of attacks carried out using drones, they are being used for infiltration or for spying. Drones have been used to spread terrorism as they can be modified to do all sorts of task’s, be it capturing a video or detonating a bomb. Attacks carried out using Drones are very dangerous as the operator is hard to locate, and can be carried out very silently.


As drones are a new concept in the aeronautical industry there are no rigid and foolproof laws which restrict the use of drones and their parameters. There are loopholes which are being taken advantage of. There is legislative uncertainty in the use of drones. There should be tracking devices in every drone made for commercial use and accompanied by a proper document to certify the flyer of the drone like a driving license. There should be proper rules like in the case of airplanes. A special unit of the ATC should be created to continuously track only drones. Drones are a boon to us by the modern aeronautical industry but they can become a bane too. So, we can expect many and frequently modified laws on the use of drones in the upcoming years.

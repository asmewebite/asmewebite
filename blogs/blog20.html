Why are Billionaires interested in space?

- By Joel Joseph

We all saw Jeff Bezos, one of the world’s richest people, just had his best day after traveling hundreds of miles above Earth’s surface in a spaceship. Before him, we saw Richard Branson’s Virgin Galactic Company also take a space flight. So why are these billionaires so interested in space?

Each of these billionaires claim to have been enchanted by the ideas of space travel ever since childhood, other than that in terms of business. One reason all those billionaires are spending billions in the aerospace industry is the profit, in that sector alone. So let's shift our focus to SpaceX, founded in 2002. It is an American space transportation services, aerospace manufacturer, and communications company. This revolved around the notion of reducing space transportation costs, hence enabling the colonization of Mars. Now, SpaceX is valued at $74 billion in 2021!

There are also several smaller private companies that have shown interest in the largely unexploited space business. By some estimates, investment in commercial space ventures has doubled from last one year alone. Even the public market has been tapped in to fund space travel.

This begs the question, instead of spending billions of dollars paying for an exclusive space flight, why can’t they help in ways that will benefit in our present time hardships like securing vaccines for billions of poor people or saving millions of people from starving. So, it will not affect day-to-day budgeting of billionaires. Or on the other hand, why don’t we make sustainable development on our earth itself, so that we can share our resources to our future generation. Why move to other planets, at all?

Supporters of these privately funded exploitation of space, debated that many technologies such as cars and telephones were once seen as symbols of social status too before they eventually became mass products. Also, the aeroplanes which we travel today were the product of privately funded efforts which benefited mankind. Another reason is that there will soon be a time when the resources on earth get depleted, which can lead to a catastrophe leading to a new habitat on a new planet.

Setting up human colonies on other planets will also bring into issues such as distinction of property rights. Many have already warned about the risk of a few billionaires establishing a region in space, but others believe that even such colonisation does not cause any risk. In order to employ their own dominance, governments should try to regulate private companies from setting up such territories.

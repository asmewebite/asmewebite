Robots in Medicines

- by Prashant

Introduction

Technology around us is changing at a rapid rate. Everyday something is emerging in the market which is making our life much easier, which helps us carry out our work in a more efficient manner. Moreover, it is also reducing human interference required to execute tasks. Same is the case with the medical field. It is appalling to know that lakhs of people die all over the world just because of medical errors, some of which are likely preventable.

This becomes a very good reason for engineers to build a robot which can prevent these mistakes which might look small but aren't. Robots are tireless and they can perform precise movements better than human hands. Here is some insight into how we can use robots to reduce human error and save lives of people.





TYPES OF ROBOTS IN MEDICAL FIELD:

• SURGICAL ROBOTS: This technology helps the surgeon to control the arm of the robot through a computer. The robot generally consists of a camera arm along with a mechanical arm consisting of the equipements. The computer console provides the surgeon a better view of the site and control than the traditional one.

For instance, ‘Da Vinci’. It is one of the most famous surgical robots. It helps in minimal invasive surgery, this in turn helps faster recovery of the patient. However, it should be noted that the robot does not perform the surgery. It is the surgeon who performs the surgery with the help of the console. It provides a magnified, 3D high definition view of the surgical area.



• EXOSKELETON: This is an electromechanical device that helps wheelchair patients to walk, stand and climb stairs. This reduces the involvement of the physiotherapist as they don't have to assist the patient, and also reduces the energy required by the patient to move their joints as now that has been managed by the electromechanical device. It also makes the patient more independent and free to move on their own. The exoskeleton works with the help of actuators.

An example of this is the Hybrid Assistive Limb(HAL). It is the world's first wearable cyborg(a fictional person). We are able to move our muscles when our brain sends signals to the muscles. These signals are also available on our skin as bioelectric signals(BES) which are sensed by the sensors attached in the HAl and that tries to coordinate with the motion of the patient



• CARE ROBOTS: Everyone on this earth is not privileged with a family. Hence for such people engineers have created a care robot which will take care of them post surgery. They will help them get into and out of the bed. They are expected to perform more complex tasks like reminding the patient to take medicines, record temperature, their hygiene, providing emotional support and also interacting with them.

On this idea, the PARO robot was developed by the Japanese. It is one of the most advanced interactive robots in the world. It is a therapeutic robot, covered in soft white fur and performs some of the functions as a real pet. It consists of 5 types of sensors: tactile, light, temperature, audition and posture sensors.



• HOSPITAL ROBOTS: In this world of developing technology, it is not justified for humans to perform tasks which can be easily carried out by robots, especially in hospitals where there should be regular observation of the patient. Hence robots have been developed for the same. They can now deliver medicines and laboratory specimens within a hospital environment, so that the nurses can be with the patients all the time. These robots can also be used to clean the hospital.

One such robot is the ‘TUG AETHON’. It performs the job of delivering food and other items and complete various transporting tasks. This reduces the load of the hospital staff and helps them focus more on the admitted patient and look after them more efficiently. It has a payload of 1000 pounds. It has a lot of sensors like the sonar, infrared sensors which helps them move safely in the hospital and prevents collision with the hospital staff and visitors.



CONCLUSION

Robots are made by humans to make their life easier. In the last 10 years, the rapid increase of Iot has made it possible for robots to assist us in every sphere of our life. Same is the case with the medical field, and so this is how robots appeared in this field as well. They provide several advantages such as high precision and better comfort.

Doctors all over the world are looking very optimistic with this development and are trying to promote it. They are even hoping for the emergence of more advanced robots which can help them in surgery, and engineers all over the world are working day and night to achieve this. These operations are expensive at this point of time, but they are estimated to be cheaper over the period of time, and everyone will have access to this facility.

Thus beyond everything, robotics will definitely help to improve the future of medicine, as it does now ,but it will happen step by step while overcoming all the actual obstacles.

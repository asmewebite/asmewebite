An Overview of BIOMECHANICS in Sports

- by Varshini Sathyamurthy

What is Biomechanics?

The term Biomechanics combines bio, which means life and mechanics, which is the study of the action of forces. The term “biomechanics” is used to describe the application of mechanics to biological systems. It can be defined in simple words as science involving the study of the mechanical aspects of living organisms or as the application of mechanical principles in the study of living organisms.

Biomechanics in Sports:

Sports biomechanics involves a detailed analysis of sports movements to minimize the risk of injury and improve athletic performance. It refers to the description, complete assessment and analysis of the movements of the human body during sport activities. In simpler words, sports biomechanics is a science that explains how and why the human body moves that way.


What is the Importance of Biomechanics in Sports?

  • It improves sports performance as Biomechanics tells us the right technique to get effective and efficient results with minimal strength and maximum results.
  • Biomechanics helps to improve new techniques, which gives better results.
  • It definitely improves the training as the coaches can alter their way of training the athletes on the basis of scientific knowledge.
  • Using biomechanics will prevent injuries as It helps us to know the forces that can lead to the injuries during the game situation.

Newton’s Laws of motion in Biomechanics:

When we talk about exploring the area of biomechanics and human movements , it is useful to consider the three laws of Newton.

  • Newton’s First Law: An object in motion will tend to stay in motion and an object at rest will tend to stay at rest unless acted upon by a force. For example, the body of an athlete sprinting down the field will tend to stay in motion unless muscular forces can overcome the inertia.


  • Newton’s Second Law: This law states that every action has an equal and opposite reaction. For instance, the force created by the legs pushing against the ground has a reaction that the ground pushes back and helps the player move forward in the ground.

  • Newton’s Third Law: It precisely explains how much motion will be experienced by a force. For example, When a ball is thrown or kicked, it tends to travel in the direction of the line of action of the applied force. Similarly, the greater the amount of force applied, the greater the speed the ball has.

Momentum is also related to Newton's second law. Linear momentum is created when an athlete sprints in a straight track. In tennis, the angular momentum generated by the coordinated action of the body segments is transmitted to the linear momentum of the racket at the time of impact.




Center of Gravity:

Center of Gravity can be defined as the point at which all the parts of an object are equally balanced.

In the case of humans, the center of gravity can change depending on the position of their body.

As the image shows, the center of gravity of a normal human being standing straight is around the area of their navel. Whereas a person stretching their hands above has the center of gravity above their navel and a person bending down has center of gravity below.

Balance of the players:

Balance is the player's ability to control their equilibrium or stability. The balance of a person is directly proportional to the size of the base. Their balance will depend on their weight. Greater the weight, more the balance. If the center of gravity is lower, the balance of the person is more.

Static balance is the ability to maintain stability and orientation when the body is at rest and the center of gravity is above the base of support. Dynamic balance is the ability to maintain stability and orientation when the body is in motion and the center of gravity is above the base of support.

Correct Biomechanics:

As mentioned above, proper biomechanics can ensure efficient movement and reduce the risk of injury but improper technique can cause abnormal biomechanics that can lead to injuries. For example, in cricket, mixed bowling actions can lead to stress fractures. An athlete can face hamstring injuries due to Anterior pelvic tilt.


Injury Management using Biomechanics:

Understanding that the shoulder can be the biomechanical cause of, for example, pelvic injuries, and vice versa, is a major advance in the treatment of injuries and back pain. Therefore, if there is an injury that does not respond to treatment, biomechanical stress elsewhere in the body may be putting pressure on this vulnerable area. Finding biomechanical causes can be important in returning to work and exercise and minimizing the risk of the same injury occurring again.

Regular sports massages help prevent injuries and injuries caused by overuse. Massage should play a very important role in any exercise program used by athletes and recreational athletes. Sports massage helps in relaxation and works perfectly with the biomechanics re-aligning process.

All sports have specific biomechanics that play a critical role in determining their performance and the way they are played. Running, cycling, baseball, tennis, badminton and swimming all have different biomechanics that can be followed to improve performance and technique. A proper coach skilled in biomechanics can teach these. This will also help in preventing numerous injuries that are generally encountered in sports and exercise. Simple factors like speed or angle or turn may lead to major injuries that can be avoided by having a proper knowledge of these biomechanics.
